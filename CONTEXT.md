# Azuero Kairós - Product Context

## What It Is

Azuero Kairós is an **Atlas de decisión alimentaria para Azuero**.

It evaluates whether Copernicus evidence is sufficient to support one of three decision states:

- **Interpretar**: evidence is sufficient for cautious interpretation with explicit limits.
- **Revisar**: evidence exists but needs review, context, or territorial verification.
- **No inferir**: evidence is not sufficient for a responsible inference.

The product is a decision system, not a data scraper.

## What It Is Not

Azuero Kairós does not detect:

- contamination
- water safety
- pesticides
- pathogens
- heavy metals
- chemical conditions
- validated crises

It does not replace laboratory analysis, authorized inspection, or human territorial judgment.

## Who It Supports

Azuero Kairós supports:

- territorial technicians
- public institutions
- food security actors
- producers
- technical reviewers
- decision makers who need a defensible next step

Its primary output is not a raw dataset. Its primary output is a decision brief that explains what can be interpreted, what must be reviewed, and what must not be inferred.

## Core Principle

Add a data layer only if it does at least one of these things:

- changes a decision
- changes a priority
- explains uncertainty

If a layer only adds technical decoration, it does not belong in the product.

## Product Thesis

In food-security decisions, a bad inference is also a risk.

Azuero Kairós protects users from overclaiming under uncertainty. Its value is not constant alerts. Its value is knowing when the evidence is strong enough to interpret and when the responsible answer is to wait, review, or verify in the field.

## Decision Posture

Every layer must answer one of these questions:

- Can this observation be interpreted?
- Should a technician review it?
- Should territorial verification be prioritized?
- Is the evidence insufficient?
- What uncertainty explains the decision?

Any layer that cannot answer one of these questions should remain outside the user-facing system.
