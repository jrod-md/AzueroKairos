# Product Positioning

PRE-HACKATHON PLANNING / DO NOT SUBMIT.

## One-Liner

AgroShield converts Copernicus Sentinel-2 evidence into a Satellite Confidence Semaforo for agricultural water-risk screening in Azuero.

## Positioning Statement

For technical agricultural operators and institutions managing irrigation and food-safety risk near Rio La Villa, AgroShield is a Copernicus-derived decision-support tool that shows whether satellite evidence is trustworthy enough to support a cautious hydro-sedimentary screening. Unlike generic dashboards, AgroShield separates evidence confidence from risk interpretation and refuses to infer when data are weak.

## Target Users

- Primary user: technical/agricultural operator.
- Secondary users: producer association, municipal/environmental institution, irrigation board, agro-export compliance team.
- Tertiary user: jury or funder evaluating whether a Copernicus workflow can become locally useful.

## Job-To-Be-Done

When a risky water/sediment condition is suspected, help the operator decide whether Copernicus evidence is usable, whether local verification is recommended, and whether the signal should be treated as normal, watch, review, or do not infer.

## Why Now

Food security systems increasingly need low-cost, transparent screening layers. Field sampling is essential but sparse; Copernicus can reduce blind spots when interpreted with confidence labels and strict limitations.

## Why Copernicus

Copernicus provides open Sentinel-2 L2A observations, a Catalog API, and Statistical API workflows that can be reproduced. The MVP can run from cached Copernicus-derived outputs and optionally refresh one date/index live.

## Why Panama / Azuero

Azuero has agricultural exposure, water stress, and public relevance around Rio La Villa. A single-node MVP is credible: it is local enough to explain and narrow enough to avoid generic regional claims.

## Why Not Just Dashboard

A map dashboard shows color. AgroShield shows whether the satellite evidence deserves trust. The product decision is not "where is red on a map"; it is "should we infer, review, or wait?"

## Competitive Differentiation

- Evidence-first, not map-first.
- Confidence state before risk state.
- Claim Firewall prevents unsafe language.
- Corridor-wide AOI balances signal stability and scientific defensibility.
- Cached reproducibility avoids live-demo failure.
- AI is communication-only, not risk calculation.
